Jackfruit

Botanical Name: Artocarpus heterophyllus
Family:Moraceae
Native Region: India and Southeast Asia
Significance: Largest tree-borne fruit in the world; economically and nutritionally important.
Uses: Fruit pulp, seeds, bark, and leaves used for food and medicine.

The Jackfruit tree, scientifically known as Artocarpus heterophyllus, is a tropical species native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia. It is best known for producing the largest tree-borne fruit in the world. Jackfruit is widely used in traditional medicine systems. Its unripe fruit is a meat substitute; seeds are rich in protein; bark and leaves have antimicrobial properties. The tree supports biodiversity, provides food and shelter to animals, and helps reduce carbon dioxide. Its deep roots also help improve soil structure and prevent erosion.
