The Oak tree, scientifically classified under the genus Quercus, includes hundreds of species found mainly in the Northern Hemisphere. Oaks are renowned for their strength, longevity, and cultural symbolism. Oak bark has traditional medicinal uses due to its astringent and anti-inflammatory properties. Acorns serve as food for various animals, and oak wood is prized for construction and furniture. Ecologically, oak trees support a wide variety of insects, birds, and mammals. Their extensive root systems help prevent soil erosion and improve forest biodiversity.
